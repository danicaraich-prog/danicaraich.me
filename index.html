DANICA RAICH

I am currently a J.D. candidate at the University of Pittsburgh School of Law, where I am pursuing the International and Comparative Law Certificate. My academic interests focus on the legal frameworks that govern international conflict, diplomacy, and transnational security challenges. I am particularly interested in how international law and national security policy interact to shape government decision-making.

Before law school, I studied philosophy and international affairs, with research projects examining topics such as nuclear weapons strategy and international accountability for human rights violations.

Outside of the classroom, I am interested in the intersection of law, military strategy, and public policy. I plan to pursue a career in public service in roles that involve international law and national security.
